I

The Comrades' Secret

“ I thought of the strange, almost mystical feeling of two people who love each other — how, from being merely ordinary, their world becomes extraordinary. ”
— Erich Maria Remarque, Three Comrades

In Remarque's tale, three devoted friends — Robert, Otto, and Gottfried — navigate a world in turmoil. They fix cars by day and dream by night. But Robert discovers something more powerful than any friendship or any engine:

He falls deeply, irrevocably in love.

She was fragile yet fierce, a light in a darkening world. Their love story is one of literature's most tender and heartbreaking.

What was the name of the woman Robert loved?

Not quite... think about the name Robert whispered to her

★ Her full name is Patricia, but Robert always called her by a shorter, sweeter name...
II

The Scent of Love

“ The fragrance drifted through the evening air like a whispered memory — delicate, intoxicating, impossible to hold. ”
— Erich Maria Remarque, Three Comrades

Every love story has a scent that defines it. For Pat, it was the fragrance of spring itself.

She adored these blossoms — clusters of tiny flowers in shades of purple and violet, their sweet perfume filling the evening air. They bloom in spring, brief and beautiful, just like the fleeting, precious moments Pat and Robert shared.

These flowers, with their heart-shaped leaves and intoxicating scent, were Pat's absolute favorites — a symbol of first love and the bittersweet beauty of things that don't last forever.

What were Pat's beloved flowers?

Not quite... think purple, fragrant, blooming in spring clusters...

★ Purple clusters, heavenly spring scent — they share a name with a color...
III

The Chariot of Fate

“ The night was dark, the city was glittering, and she was sitting beside me — and I knew right then that nothing in my life would ever be the same. ”
— Erich Maria Remarque, Three Comrades

Every great love story has a moment when fate intervenes.

For Robert and Pat, that moment came on four wheels. The three comrades had poured their hearts into restoring an old racing car — battered on the outside, but with the soul of a champion beneath the hood.

They loved this car so much they gave it a human name, as if it were a fourth member of their brotherhood. And it was in this very car, on a moonlit drive through the city, that Robert first sat beside Pat — the night everything began.

What name did the three comrades give their beloved car?

Not quite... it's a short, classic name — one you'd give a loyal old friend.

★ A strong, simple German name that starts with K...
IV

The Dreamer's Farewell

“ Love life — even when it gives you nothing but sorrow. Because life itself is the only real treasure. ”
— Erich Maria Remarque, Three Comrades

Of the three comrades, one was the eternal romantic — the dreamer, the philosopher, the one who saw beauty where others saw ruin.

He believed in grand gestures, in the power of words, in living fully and recklessly. He was the first to toast to love, the first to defend a friend, and the one whose absence left the deepest silence.

His death was a turning point in the story — a moment that reminded Robert how fragile everything is, and how fiercely we must hold on to those we love.

What was the first name of this dreamer, the most romantic of the three comrades?

Not Robert, not Otto... the third comrade, the poetic soul.

★ His surname was Lenz. His first name means "God's peace" in German...
V

The Art of Taming

“ You become responsible, forever, for what you have tamed. ”
— Antoine de Saint-Exupéry, The Little Prince

Now we turn the page to another story of love — told through the eyes of a child from the stars.

In his journey across the planets, the Little Prince meets a wise creature who teaches him the most important lesson of all: "To tame means to create bonds."

This creature explains that before they are tamed, they are just one among thousands. But once tamed, they become unique in all the world to each other. The wheat fields will forever remind him of the Prince's golden hair.

It is this creature who whispers the secret: "What is essential is invisible to the eye — you can only see it with the heart."

What animal taught the Little Prince about love and belonging?

Not quite... this clever, bushy-tailed creature is known for its cunning...

★ A small, clever animal often seen in fables — it rhymes with "box"...
VI

The One Among Thousands

“ It is the time you have wasted for your rose that makes your rose so important. ”
— Antoine de Saint-Exupéry, The Little Prince

On his tiny planet, the Little Prince devoted himself to one single, beautiful, and rather vain companion.

She had four thorns she believed would protect her from the world. She coughed dramatically to make him feel guilty. She was proud, difficult, and sometimes exhausting.

And yet — he loved her. He watered her, shielded her from the wind, and crossed the entire universe only to understand that she was unique among all others of her kind, simply because she was his.

What was the Little Prince's beloved companion on his planet?

Not quite... think of something beautiful, with thorns, that grows from the ground...

★ Beautiful, fragrant, with thorns — the classic symbol of love...
VII

The Emperor's City

Now our quest leaves the world of books and enters the world of ancient history.

There exists a city that has witnessed two thousand years of love, faith, and wisdom unfold along the shores of a great water.

This city was founded around 20 CE by Herod Antipas, who named it in honor of the Roman Emperor who ruled the known world.

It became one of the four holy cities of the Jewish people. Within its walls, the great scholars composed the Jerusalem Talmud, shaping the wisdom of generations.

The great philosopher and physician Maimonides chose this city as his eternal resting place.

What is this ancient and holy city?

Not quite... the Emperor's name was Tiberius. The city bears his name.

★ The Emperor's name was Tiberius... and the city still carries his legacy.
VIII

The Harp-Shaped Sea

Wonderful! You've found the city. But every great love story needs a setting by the water.

This city sits along the western shore of Israel's most beloved lake — a body of water that has nourished civilizations for millennia.

The ancients named it for its shape, which resembles a harp (kinnor in Hebrew). Its waters are sweet, its sunsets legendary, and its stories infinite.

Jesus walked upon its waves. Fishermen have cast their nets here since before the time of kings.

At 209 meters below sea level, it is the lowest freshwater lake on Earth.

What is the Hebrew name of this legendary sea?

Almost there... think about "kinnor" (harp) and add the place suffix...

★ Named after the Hebrew word for "harp" — kinnor...
