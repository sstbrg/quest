II

The Emperor's City

Like the love between Robert and Pat — timeless and enduring — there exists a city that has witnessed two thousand years of history unfold along the shores of a great water.

This city was founded around 20 CE by Herod Antipas, who named it in honor of the Roman Emperor who ruled the known world.

It became one of the four holy cities of the Jewish people. Within its walls, the great scholars composed the Jerusalem Talmud, shaping the wisdom of generations.

The great philosopher and physician Maimonides chose this city as his eternal resting place.

What is this ancient and holy city?

★ Hint: The Emperor's name was Tiberius... and the city still carries his legacy.
Not quite... the Emperor's name was Tiberius. The city bears his name.
III

The Harp-Shaped Sea

Wonderful! You've found the city. But every great city has a great body of water that gives it life.

Tiberias sits along the western shore of Israel's most beloved lake — a body of water that has nourished civilizations for millennia.

The ancients named it for its shape, which resembles a harp (kinnor in Hebrew). Its waters are sweet, its sunsets legendary, and its stories infinite.

Jesus walked upon its waves. Fishermen have cast their nets here since before the time of kings.

At 209 meters below sea level, it is the lowest freshwater lake on Earth.

What is the Hebrew name of this legendary sea?

★ Hint: Named after the Hebrew word for "harp" — kinnor...
Almost there... think about "kinnor" (harp) and add the place suffix...
